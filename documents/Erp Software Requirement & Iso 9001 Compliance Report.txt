ERP Software Requirement Specification
For Trading Company – Oil, Gas, Power & Petrochemical Industries
1. Objective of the Software
The objective of this software is to develop an integrated ERP system for a trading company dealing in Stainless Steel, Carbon Steel, Alloy Steel, Nickel & Copper Alloys, Tubulars, Structurals, Pipe Fittings, Flanges, Valves, Instrumentation, Mechanical & Electrical Components for Oil, Gas, Power & Petrochemical Industries.
The system should: - Control end-to-end business processes - Ensure traceability from enquiry to delivery & payment - Support quality assurance & documentation - Generate MIS for management decisions - Fully comply with ISO 9001:2018 Quality Management System requirements

2. General System Requirements (Common Across All Modules)
These points must be communicated clearly to the software development team.
2.1 User & Access Control
Role-based access (Admin, Sales, Purchase, QC, Stores, Accounts, Management)
User-wise activity logging (audit trail)
Password policy & login history
2.2 Master Data Management
Customer Master
Supplier/Vendor Master
Product Master (Material grade, size, standard, schedule, heat no., manufacturer)
Tax Master (GST, IGST, export tax)
Unit of Measure (Nos, Mtrs, Kg, MT)
Payment terms, delivery terms, inspection agency master
2.3 Document Control (ISO 9001 Clause 7.5)
Auto document numbering
Revision control
Approval workflows
Controlled templates for quotations, POs, invoices, MTCs
Soft copy storage with version history
2.4 Traceability (Critical for ISO 9001)
Traceability from:
Enquiry → Quotation → Sales Order → Purchase Order → Incoming Inspection → Inventory → Dispatch → Invoice → Payment
Heat No. / Batch No. level traceability

4. ISO 9001:2018 Specific Mandatory Requirements to Inform Developer
The software must support the following ISO clauses:
Clause 4.4: Process interaction mapping
Clause 5.3: Roles & responsibilities
Clause 6.1: Risk identification (delivery delay, supplier delay)
Clause 7.2: Competence (user role mapping)
Clause 7.5: Documented information control
Clause 8: Operational planning & control
Clause 9: Internal audit & MIS
Clause 10: Corrective action tracking

5. Audit & Compliance Features
Complete audit trail for every transaction
Change history with user, date & reason
Data export for ISO audit
Access to historical records (minimum 5–7 years)

6. Technology & Security Recommendations
Web-based ERP (cloud or on-premise)
Database with backup & disaster recovery
SSL encryption
Daily automated backups
API support for future integrations

7. Deliverables Expected from Software Development Team

8. Conclusion
This ERP software should not only manage business operations but also act as a Quality Management System (QMS) support tool, ensuring compliance with ISO 9001:2018 and providing full traceability, control, and continuous improvement capabilities.
